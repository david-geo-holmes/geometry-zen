Geometry Zen

Looking at the universe from a Geometric Algebra perspective


Geometry Zen is an online tool for students of Physics, Engineering, and Computer Graphics. It provides Hands-on Learning of Geometric Algebra using Python Programming and 3D Visualization. It is the goal of this tool to make Geometric Algebra familiar to young science students, undergraduates, researchers and educators so that Geometric Algebra can become the standard Unified Mathematical Language for Physics and Engineering in the 21st Century. It is 100% free, Open Source Software, and no registration is required.

What is Geometric Algebra?

Geometric Algebra is the generalization of the concept of a number to describe geometric quantities and defines the rules for manipulating those numbers. In essence, Geometric Algebra is about learning how to correctly multiply and interpret Geometric Numbers.

Many of the Physical Laws that describe the phenomena of nature are Geometric and Algebraic in Character. That is to say, the laws have a precise mathematical structure that is amenable to a geometric interpretation. Geometric Algebra unifies these two aspects in a single mathematical tool. By learning and using Geometric Algebra (and Geometric Calculus) the student gains a new perspective and a lasting foundation for understanding the universe and making new discoveries.

Alternatives to Geometric Algebra

Complex Numbers, Quaternions, Matrices, Tensors, Differential Geometry...

All of these tools are subsumed by Geometric Algebra which also reveals their underlying, but sometimes hidden, geometric interpretation.

A Brief History of Geometric Numbers

  1. 1545 Gerolamo Cardano uses numbers less than zero and acknowledges the existence of imaginary numbers. We now understand that complex numbers are isomorphic to the even sub-algebra of the Geometric algebra of the plane.
  2. 1637 Rene Descartes initiates the union of geometry and algebra.
  3. 1840 Herman Gunter Grassman submits essay containing the first known appearance of linear algebra and the concept of a vector space.
  4. 1843 William Rowan Hamilton invents quaternions. We now understand that quaternions are related to the even sub-algebra of the Geometric algebra of ordinary Euclidean three-dimensional space.
  5. 1844 Grassman publishes Die Lineale Ausdenungslehre, ein neuer Zweig der Mathematik (The Theory of Linear Extension, a New Branch of Mathematics). His work shows that there are useful geometric numbers that generalize scalars and vectors.
  6. 1870 Josiah Willard Gibbs and Oliver Heaviside idependently invent vector calculus.
  7. 1878 William Kingdon Clifford expands Grassman's Ausdehnungslehre and Hamilton's quaternions by defining the geometric product.
  8. 1879 Clifford dies at age 33.
  9. 1880 Gibbs advocates use of 3D Vector analysis over quaternions.
  10. 1966 David Orlin Hestenes publishes Space Time Algebra and reinterprets the Pauli and Dirac matrices.
